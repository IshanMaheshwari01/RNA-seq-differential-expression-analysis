Executive Summary

Study Overview

This comprehensive RNA-seq analysis compares tumor tissue versus normal tissue, identifying 3,296 differentially expressed genes and revealing key biological pathways dysregulated in cancer.

Findings

  • 3,296 genes significantly differentially expressed (16.7% of transcriptome)
  • 1,654 genes upregulated in tumor → proliferation pathways
  • 1,642 genes downregulated in tumor → immune pathways
  • Fold changes ranging from 0.04x to 28.1x
  • P-values as low as 1.36×10⁻¹⁴

Upregulated Pathways

Cell cycle, DNA replication, mitosis

→ Indicates uncontrolled cell proliferation (cancer hallmark)

Downregulated Pathways

Immune response, defense mechanisms

→ Indicates immune evasion (cancer hallmark)

Key Findings Summary

Molecular Changes

  • Proliferation activated: 95 genes in cell cycle pathway, up to 28-fold upregulation
  • Immune system suppressed: 89 genes in immune response pathway, down to 0.04-fold (96% reduction)
  • Result: Tumor shows aggressive phenotype with immune evasion

Clinical Implications

  • Therapeutic targets: Cell cycle inhibitors (CDK4/6 inhibitors)
  • Immunotherapy potential: Restore immune function (checkpoint inhibitors)
  • Prognosis: Proliferative signature suggests aggressive tumor requiring multi-modal treatment
